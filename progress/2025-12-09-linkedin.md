Here's the uncomfortable truth nobody's talking about in AI search optimization:

Most websites obsessing over AI visibility are invisible to everyone.

I built an AI search analysis tool. It tells you how ready your site is for ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity to cite you.

Then I noticed a pattern that made me question everything.

Sites kept scoring around 67%. Good structured data. Decent content.

But when I dug deeper? No SEO foundation.

Missing meta descriptions.
No internal linking.
HTTP instead of HTTPS.
URLs that looked auto-generated.

Let me do the math:

- AI search: ~5% of total search traffic
- Traditional Google: ~95% of search traffic
- Sites with basic SEO issues: ~70% of SMB websites

We're optimizing 70% of websites for 5% of traffic while they're hemorrhaging the other 95%.

That's not optimization. That's rearranging deck chairs.

So today I shipped something different:

AImpactScanner now runs TWO audits in one scan:

AI Readiness (18 factors)
SEO Foundation (9 factors)

27 factors. 15 seconds. One scan.

Because you can't win the AI race if Google can't even find you.

The foundation has to exist before the optimization matters.

What's a feature you almost didn't build that turned out to be essential?
