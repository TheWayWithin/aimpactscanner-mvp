We audited our own marketing claims yesterday. The results were uncomfortable.

Our codebase had FIVE different answers to "how many factors does your analysis cover?"

27 in architecture.md.
22 in the pricing page.
18 in the README.
All stated with confidence.
None matching the actual code.

The truth: 18 scored factors across 8 pillars, plus 9 foundational SEO checks that run but aren't part of the framework score.

Worse: our core competitive claim -- "the only AI search diagnostic with a published methodology" -- was technically false. The methodology docs exist internally. They were never published publicly.

The lesson for any founder building a SaaS product:

Your documentation drifts. Every sprint, every marketing push, every stakeholder deck rounds up numbers slightly. Over months, you end up with multiple "truths" about your own product.

The fix: regular audits where you trace every claim back to the source code. If the code says 18, it's 18. Not "approximately 27."

Now we're fixing it. Every number aligned. Every claim verifiable.

Has anyone else caught their own marketing contradicting their product?
